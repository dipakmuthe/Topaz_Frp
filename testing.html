Testing & Quality Assurance

We deploy rigorous chemical and mechanical verification protocols to ensure structural reliability under extreme loading.

Our Standards

End-to-End Audits

Topaz FRP leads the industry by implementing analytical chemical testing directly on raw polymer matrices.

GC Testing

Gas chromatography (GC) is used in analytical chemistry to separate and analyze compounds that can be vaporized. We run all resin and release agents through GC to analyze purity prior to production—we are the only player in the industry conducting this quality control.

Tensile Testing

Tensile testing is a fundamental engineering test where a sample is subjected to controlled tension until failure. We measure ultimate tensile strength, breaking strength, and max elongation of raw fiber mats. This ensures our internal reinforcement framework exceeds loading guidelines.

Load Testing

Covers are tested for load bearing and permanent set deformation as per BS EN 124:1994/2015 and IS 1726 standards. Permanent set testing applies multiple short-duration compression loads to confirm the cover does not warp or crack under vehicle impact.

Workflow

Laboratory Testing Pipeline

Follow our systematic, scroll-active QA checkpoints from raw composite chemistry to final load certifications.

1

Raw Purity

Resin and catalyst matrices are vaporized in Gas Chromatography to audit chemical purity.

2

Fiber Tensile

Tensile load tension is applied to glass fiber reinforcement strands to verify breaking thresholds.

3

Hot Compression

Molding compounds are compressed under immense heat to cross-link polymers and remove air pockets.

4

Load Press

Calibrated load blocks press into covers at the proof limit (up to 40T) to test sag and elasticity.

5

Batch Release

Batch validation sheets are signed, standard marks are embossed, and fleet container dispatch begins.
